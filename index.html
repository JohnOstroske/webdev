The Internet Engineering Task Force

The Internet Engineering Task Force is, as far as I can tell, an international organization made up largely of volunteers that are broken into subgroups and organizations that research and develop Internet infrastructure.

The groups include:

  • Internet Engineering Steering Group
  • Internet Research Task Force
  • IETF Trust
  • Tools Team
  • Internet Architecture Board
  • IETF Administration and Operations Committee
  • Nominations Committee
  • Directorates

You can find more details on the IETF website
https://www.ietf.org/

Better privacy through a new DNS protocol

One major initiative being researched by the IETF is a new protocol used to make it harder to track pages you have visited.

Currently, it is very easy to Internet Service Providers and Search Engines to track and log the websites you visit by tracking your DNS history. While these organizations cannot see what you are doing on those sites, they can see what sites you visit.

An organization called Cloudflare is attempting to become the first to release a service, called 1.1.1.1, that utilizes the new DNS standards being created by the IETF. While it is still in a testing phase, it does look promising.

You can learn more about this initiative in this Wider article:
https://www.wired.com/story/new-encryption-service-adds-privacy-protection-for-web-browsing/

The Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers

ICANN in simple terms, is an organization that manages Internet computer addressing.

There are billions of devices connected to the Internet, and each must have its own address. Furthermore, each must be able to connect to and communicate with billions of other devices on the Internet. This is done through the Domain Name System (DNS).

DNS is the system that links names (such as www.google.com, or my.tri-c.edu) to specific IP addresses. ICANN is the organization that assigns and manages those address. Just think of where the Internet would be if this wasn't possible!

You can find more details on the ICANN website
https://www.icann.org/

Need for privacy vs need for information...

It seems that ICANN has found itself in a bit of a tough situation. The EU has released new, stricter data protection regulations called GDPR. ICANN stores names, addresses, phone numbers, etc... of everybody who owns a WebSite domain. As it can probably be deduced, this creates a bit of a conundrum...

It will be very interesting to see how these organizations agree to balance the need to protect people's data with the need to store certain information...
